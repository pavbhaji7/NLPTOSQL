# 1.1 Introduction to Project

We live in an era entirely defined by data. Look under the hood of almost any modern enterprise—whether it’s a hospital managing patient records, a retail giant tracking inventory, or a bank handling millions of daily transactions—and you will find a relational database. These databases are incredibly efficient at storing and organizing massive amounts of information. However, there is a massive catch: to actually get answers out of them, you have to speak their language. For decades, that language has been Structured Query Language (SQL). 

While SQL is undeniably powerful, it creates a steep learning curve that locks out the vast majority of business professionals, managers, and domain experts from directly interacting with their own data. To fix this broken workflow, the software industry and academic researchers have invested heavily in Natural Language Interfaces to Databases (NLIDB). The ultimate dream is straightforward: let a user type a question in everyday English, and have the computer automatically fetch the right numbers. This project seeks to bring that dream closer to a safe, reliable reality by building an NL2SQL translation pipeline.
