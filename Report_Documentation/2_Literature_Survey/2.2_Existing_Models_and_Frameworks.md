# 2.2 Existing Models and Frameworks

In the earlier days of NL2SQL research, the dominant strategy was to use syntactic parsing and rigid rule-based synthesis. A notable example of this era is the work done on Sqlizer, where researchers attempted to synthesize queries by mapping natural language phrases directly to SQL fragments using predefined semantic rules. 

As computing power grew and neural networks became the academic standard, the entire field underwent a massive paradigm shift. As outlined in extensive surveys on the topic, the focus moved aggressively toward deep learning. Researchers began treating text-to-SQL essentially as a foreign language translation problem—using sequence-to-sequence neural networks. This allowed systems to handle a much wider variety of sentence structures without needing hard-coded rules for every single conversational edge case.

This deep learning wave eventually culminated in the current era of Large Language Models. Frameworks leveraging models like GPT have become the norm, focusing purely on natural language interpretation to generate SQL syntax on the fly.
