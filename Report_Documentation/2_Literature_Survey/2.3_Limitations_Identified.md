# 2.3 Limitations Identified from Literature Survey (Research Gaps)

Despite massive leaps forward, the literature survey reveals significant gaps and limitations. Early rule-based systems had a fatal flaw: their rigidity. Natural language is messy, highly contextual, and unpredictable. If a user asked a question using a synonym the system hadn't been explicitly programmed to recognize, the translation would fail completely. This brittleness made them difficult to scale across different enterprise domains without constant, manual developer intervention.

On the other end of the spectrum, while LLMs brought incredible conversational flexibility, researchers quickly realized that raw generative AI was a poor fit for the rigid, unforgiving syntax of SQL. Standard LLMs often fail to grasp the underlying architecture of a database, leading to structurally invalid queries. Inferring SQL directly from natural language specifications frequently leads to "hallucinations"—where the AI confidently invents columns or misinterprets foreign-key relationships. The research gap clearly points to the lack of systems that balance conversational flexibility with deterministic structural validation.
